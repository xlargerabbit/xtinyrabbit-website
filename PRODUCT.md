# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Fellow engineers working in systems programming, developer tooling, and infrastructure. They read with scrutiny — they'll notice a cliché, a wasted word, or a design choice that doesn't hold up. They come for depth and technical precision, not personality performance.

## Product Purpose

A personal tech blog at xlargerabbit.github.io. Writing is the product. The site's job is to get out of the way and let the work speak. Success means a reader stays to the end and comes back for the next post.

## Brand Personality

Precise, spare, direct. No hedging. No decoration that doesn't earn its place. The aesthetic reference is acko.net and ciechanow.ski: technical depth as craft, explanation as a form of art. Visual complexity is permitted only when it aids comprehension.

## Anti-references

- Medium / Substack: the platform aesthetic, indistinguishable from thousands of other blogs. Social engagement widgets, "clap" energy, platform-brand contamination.
- Corporate tech blog: committee-approved, risk-neutral, brand-sanitized. Reads like no one is home.

## Design Principles

1. **Prose is the product.** Layout, color, and spacing serve reading. Decoration is a tax on attention. Every element that doesn't aid comprehension is a candidate for removal.
2. **Earn the complexity.** Visual elements must carry meaning, not mood. If a treatment exists only to look interesting, cut it.
3. **Technical precision over warmth.** Design choices should signal serious work to a fellow engineer, not approachability to a general audience.
4. **Quiet but not invisible.** Restraint isn't passivity. One deliberate choice is worth more than ten safe defaults.
5. **The writing leads.** Prose, code blocks, and diagrams take center stage. The chrome — header, nav, footer — recedes.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Serif body text requires generous line height and size — never go below 18px for prose. Full keyboard navigation. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Color contrast must pass at both light and dark themes independently.
